About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Hong Kong Catastrophic Risk Centre (HKCRC), Lingnan University. I received my PhD from the University of Hong Kong (HKU) under the supervision of David McCarthy, and my MPhil from Lingnan University under the supervision of Jiji Zhang and Simon Goldstein.


My research revolves around a central question: What should rational agents do in the face of uncertainty? This drives my work in formal epistemology and decision theory. In particular, I explore various topics in Bayesian epistemology through a decision-theoretic lens.


Beyond Bayesian epistemology, I am also deeply interested in philosophy and ethics of AI, especially the epistemological and ethical issues related to machine learning and artificial general intelligence (AGI).


You can contact me at maomeimuriel@gmail.com or visit my profile on PhilPapers and Google Scholar.
